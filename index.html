Critical

The Webinar-To-Blueprint Gap Costs The Friction-Averse Buyer

The free DSL training class is the top of the funnel. From there, the buyer either self-checks out into a Blueprint tier ($1,997 entry, $4,997 Premium, $10,497 Ultimate) or drops off. There's no mid-tier self-checkout SKU between the free training and the Blueprint Premium price point, and there's no application-call path for the buyer who'd buy a higher tier if a real human walked them through the fit. Two leaks compound. First, the warm webinar attendee who'd buy a $497-$997 advanced training (niche selection deep-dive, supplier negotiation playbook, ad-creative bank) drops off because the only paid path is the full Blueprint. Second, the qualified buyer who's allergic to self-checkout and would commit at $4,997-$10,497 with a sales conversation never enters the Premium or Ultimate tier at all. The funnel asks every buyer to commit to a self-checkout decision off a single training class, when a meaningful slice of them would convert better through a different path.

The LiftA mid-tier self-checkout SKU at $497-$997 (advanced training, no call, instant access, lower-touch fulfilment) plus an application-call path into Premium and Ultimate for buyers who need a human conversation before committing $4,997-$10,497. Sits between the webinar and the Blueprint, captures both buyers the current funnel drops, and pre-qualifies the warm Premium audience without burning trust on a hard self-checkout.

Won't the High-Ticket Operator Cohort cannibalise the AK Founder Circle?

No. The Cohort and the Founder Circle solve different problems. Founder Circle is a recurring senior community for serious operators with ongoing programming, peer access, and Anton time on a published cadence. The Cohort is a $25K-$30K application-only operating-room intensive for operators who want a 90-day implementation sprint, two days in person, and a tighter peer group. Different deliverable, different price, different buying motion. Most Cohort applicants will be Founder Circle members moving up the ladder, not net-new buyers choosing Cohort over Founder Circle. Where there's overlap, the Cohort is the explicit "post-Founder-Circle operating tier." It expands the ladder, it doesn't compete inside it.

Why would I run this with an outside operator instead of in-house?

Because the team you have is already running the business. The Blueprint is fulfilling and selling. The Founder Circle is delivering programming. The Retreat is in production. DSL X / XA is in development. Adding a 90-day ladder build, a senior-tier launch, a mid-tier self-checkout SKU, and a paid acquisition layer on top of an already-loaded ops team is how those projects either get postponed by a year or get launched at half the quality. Outside operator is faster, cleaner, and exits when production hits steady state. We hand the system to your team after the launch cycle, not before.

How much of my time does this need?

Roughly half a day to record the mid-tier SKU positioning content. One day to record the High-Ticket Operator Cohort positioning. Calendar holds for cohort delivery in Lever 02. The rest is build team work plus you reviewing the copy, creative, and pricing decisions before they ship. Most operators at your stage spend under 8 hours a month during the 90-day build, and under 2 hours a month after the system is in production.

How does this work alongside my current team?

The build team operates alongside your existing structure. Your content team keeps producing. Your customer success team keeps onboarding. Your Blueprint delivery, Founder Circle programming, Retreat planning, and DSL X / XA development all stay where they are. We layer on top: mid-tier SKU, application-call path, cross-portfolio ladder, Cohort sales engine, paid layer, Founder Circle retention. The only meaningful interface point is Anton on camera for the positioning content. Everything else, we run autonomously and report back.

What if my existing Founder Circle already covers some of what the Cohort would?

There's overlap on some content (niche selection, supplier strategy, AI tooling), but the deliverable is different. Founder Circle is "ongoing community access with monthly programming and Anton on a cadence." Cohort is "you already run a profitable store, here's how to operate at multi-store portfolio scale, with peer operators, in a strategic 90-day implementation format that the community format can't deliver." Most Cohort attendees will already be Founder Circle members. The overlap is feature, not bug. Cohort buys insider continuity for operators who don't want to leave the Anton ecosystem to find their next tier with another coach.
